A BRIEF INTRODUCTION

Hi there! I'm Derrick.

I’m currently a junior double majoring in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics and Statistics. I was born and raised in Queens, New York and love spending time with family and friends, traveling, and baking in my free time. Ever since I was a kid, I was amazed by how technology can be used to help anyone of any field. Helping others is always a large virtue of mine and I envisioned computer science as the perfect way to find creative and innovative solutions for everyone.

DISCOVERY

Going out of your comfort zone can do a lot.

In high school, my main motivation was to obtain good grades - it seemed to be the only factor that proved myself in front of others. However, the freedom that college life introduced to me proved me wrong. What do you want to do in the future? That was the one question that slammed everyone in the face on day one. For the first time, I couldn’t say that good grades were my future. Granted, good grades were good for the future, but for what future once I finish college? As I saw others with ambitions and dreams at school, I recognized that I wanted to find my own ambition - as silly as it sounds, I wanted to discover who I was and find dreams to chase like them, and the only way to do that is by jumping out of my comfort zone. And during the process, failing is okay. I failed club interviews, quit being a lab assistant, and loved giving technical support at my on-campus job. I learned that I needed to improve on communicating and learned what I was and was not interested in. There's so much to learn, but I'm glad that I am starting this journey of self-discovery now rather than never.

USABILITY

Technology shouldn't be hard to use for anyone.

As I learned new programs and performed tech support on the job or for my parents, something became very apparent: as far as technology has advanced, it is sometimes still hard to use. Sometimes, it baffles me that programs were made without the user in mind. Many of us tend to get ahead of ourselves by seeing the innovation that our program can bring, but that innovation cannot be used if the users themselves cannot figure out how to use it. Technology comes with a large learning curve for many users, which is usually caused by poor user interfaces, planning, and execution. Thus, I’ve been heavily invested in human-computer interaction and web/app development, making my goal to create simple, fluid, and personal experiences to help anyone of any industry. We shouldn't have to sacrifice usability with innovation. By combining these ideals with innovation, we can create an immersive canvas and build more with our clients, together.
